2014, September 26

80 students from Ayotzinapa Rural Teacher's College comandeer five buses to attend a protest in Mexico City. Due to roadblocks, the students turn around and head to the nearby city of Iguala. At the end of the night 43 out of the 80 students are unaccounted for.

Click through in the dark to find out more about what happened -->

2014, September 29th

The search for the missing 43 students officially begins.

2014, October 03

The Inter-American Commission of Human Rights steps in to help the investigation.

2014, October 08

A mass grave is found with 28 remains inside as part of the investigation. The missing students' families invite the well-respected Argentine Forensic Anthropology Team to investigate these remains. During this search for the students, more than 60 mass graves are found in and around the area of Iguala, Guerrero.

2014, November 07

Mexico's Attorney General, Murillo Karam proposes an "official" account of what occured the night of September 26th 2014. The Attorney General's Office's version of events state that the municipal police of Iguala handed over the 43 detained students to the drug cartel Guerreros Unidos, who then killed them, burnt their bodies, and dumped their ashes and other remains into the nearby San Juan River.

2014, December 08

The Attorney General's Office discovers the remains of one student, Alexander Mora, as the Mexican Commission takes the lead in the investigaton.

2015, January 27th

The Attorney General states that he considers the case solved citing his previous version of events as the "historic truth" denying military involvement.

2022, May

On the night of September 26th 2014, 43 students went missing at the hands of federal, state, and municipal forces. Corruption has prevented the families of the victims from getting justice and peace of mind, as the government put up smoke screen after smoke screen in order to protect the real perpetrators. While there is no clear motive, the families of the missing 43 continue to fight and push for answers even 8 years later. Throughout the investigation, numerous political leaders have stepped down or fled as the truth of what really happened that night, and the government's handling of the case, starts to come out, bit by bit.
